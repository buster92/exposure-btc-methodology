# Metrics and Validation Framing

## 1. Validation philosophy

Exposure BTC should be judged primarily on **risk-adjusted usefulness**, not on whether it predicts every move.

The evaluation philosophy emphasizes:
- avoiding structurally poor exposure periods
- reducing severe drawdowns
- improving long-term compounding conditions
- staying grounded in out-of-sample thinking

## 2. Why drawdown matters

Drawdown is not a secondary metric.

It is central.

A large drawdown creates both:
- a mathematical problem, because recovery requires disproportionately larger gains
- a behavioral problem, because investors often abandon discipline after major losses

Examples:
- A 20% loss requires a 25% gain to recover
- A 50% loss requires a 100% gain to recover

That is why a model focused on drawdown control can be valuable even when it does not maximize raw upside in every phase.

## 3. Public framing of evaluation

The methodology can be described publicly through metrics such as:

- **Maximum drawdown**
- **Comparison against buy-and-hold**
- **Regime stability**
- **Risk behavior during volatility expansion**
- **Walk-forward consistency**

## 4. Current public claim structure

The public positioning for Exposure BTC is centered around:
- model tracking since 2021
- walk-forward style validation mindset
- historical maximum drawdown around 8% in simulation
- focus on exposure quality rather than permanent full exposure

## 5. Why walk-forward thinking matters

A static backtest can look impressive while hiding fragility.

Walk-forward logic matters because it asks a better question:

> Does the framework remain useful as market conditions evolve?

That is more relevant for a regime-aware system than a single hindsight-optimized curve.

## 6. What should be emphasized publicly

When describing results, the strongest emphasis should stay on:
- risk control
- discipline
- structural interpretation
- reduction of bad exposure periods

Not on:
- aggressive performance promises
- exact return maximization claims
- false certainty
